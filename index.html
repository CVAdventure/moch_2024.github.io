Abstract

In the realm of Video Camouflaged Object Detection (VCOD), the scarcity of data poses a significant challenge, hampering the development and refinement of detection algorithms.

To address this critical issue, we propose a comprehensive approach that encompasses a new dataset, a synthetic data pipeline, and an effective VCOD network. Firstly, we develop a dedicated benchmark for Video Camouflaged Human Detection (VCHD), i.e., MOving Camouflaged Human (MoCH), which covers various human-centric camouflaged scenarios.Secondly, we generate a large amount of synthetic data, incorporating multi-view observations to simulate a wide range of scenarios and camouflaged conditions. This could serve as an auxiliary training source to help current VCOD methods improve robustness and versatility.Lastly, our proposed VCOD method termed Mask-MotionNet (MMNet) outperforms state-of-the-art methods by a large margin on multiple benchmarks.
